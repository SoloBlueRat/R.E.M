Background Info

An American rock band orginated in Athens, Georgia, U.S in 1980. Lead by 4 members of the band: Bill Berry, Peter Buck, Mike Mills, and Michael Stipe.

Their first peformance is at Church Steeple St. Mary's Episcopal Church at April 5, 1980. 300 people showed up in the event for a newest band group and the crowd applaud the band performance, in fact, that the crowd wanted them to play a their set a second time.

Having toured around the world, mainly to europe, it is the most popular band in the U.K. Out of Time (1991) having sold 1.7 million records in U.K alone. As Mike Mills joked "Europeans are smarter" about overseas popularity during a press conference.

Genres

Alternative Rock / post-punk / college rock

The term Alternative rock means a small group peforming for small independent studios compared to large entertainment corporations with a larger budget. This genre would be popular in college culture in the early days of R.E.M band before they gone mainstream before the release of Out of time (1991) and Automatic for the people(1992).

  • Guitarist: Peter Buck 1980-2011
  • Bassist: Mike Mills 1980-2011
  • Lead Vocalist: Michael Stipe 1980-2011
